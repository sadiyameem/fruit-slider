Apple

Banana

The banana is actually a berry, too.

Blueberry

Blueberries are one of the few fruits that are native to North America.

Orange

The most widely grown fruit in the world is the orange.

Strawberry

A single strawberry can have as many as 200 seeds.

Pineapple

Pineapples were named after a pinecone because they looked similar to the fruit.

Mango

Mangoes are the most commonly eaten fruit in the world.

Grape

Grapes can explode when microwaved.

Watermelon

Watermelons are actually a type of berry.

Kiwi

Kiwifruit used to be called “Chinese gooseberries.”

Cherry

The world’s largest cherry tree can produce up to 7,000 cherries per season.

Peach

Fruits with a single large seed or pit, such as peaches and cherries, are called “drupes.”

Pear

The world’s largest pear weighed 5 pounds and 8.8 ounces and was grown in Japan in 2012.

Lemon

A lemon contains more sugar than a strawberry.

Lime

The world’s largest lime weighed 5 pounds and 11.36 ounces and was grown in California in 2007.

Raspberry

There are over 200 different species of raspberries.

Blackberry

Mexico is the world’s largest blackberry grower.

Starfruit

The taste of this tropical fruit depends upon its varieties – sweet or tart. The sweet one tastes like a punch of pineapple and orange while the tart tastes like lemon.

Guava

The world’s largest guava weighed 5 pounds and 9 ounces and was grown in Hawaii in 2006.

Passion-fruit

The flower of the passion fruit vine is the national flower of Paraguay.

Coconut

A coconut is the largest seed in the world.

Avocado

The world’s largest avocado tree can produce up to 200 avocados per year.
